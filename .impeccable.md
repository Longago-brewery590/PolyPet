## Design Context

### Users
PolyPet serves two audiences: **library consumers** (indie game developers, hobbyists, and learners integrating procedural pets into Unity/Godot projects) and **end-users** (players and casual users who interact with the generated creatures in finished apps). The library itself has no UI -- design context applies to the visual identity of generated polygon pets and any future sample scenes or documentation.

### Brand Personality
**Cute, playful, quirky, bold, colorful, fun, simple.**
The voice is toy-like and cheerful with a geometry pun at its core ("Acute-ly"). Pets should feel immediately charming and slightly weird in an endearing way -- never generic, corporate, or dark.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual tone**: Flat geometric shapes with retro toy energy. Clean polygon silhouettes with solid saturated fills.
- **References**: Tamagotchi (toy-like simplicity, compact creatures), Peridot (procedural variety, living feel), Geometry Wars (vivid polygon rendering, electric color palette on geometric forms).
- **Anti-references**: No realistic/detailed rendering (no fur, shading, or naturalistic anatomy). No corporate mascot or stock art feel. No dark, edgy, or horror aesthetics.
- **Color direction**: Bright and saturated. Avoid muddy, dark, brown, gray, or desaturated tones. The current HSV range (sat 0.4-0.8, value 0.7-1.0) is intentionally vivid.
- **Theme**: No UI theme applies -- this is a rendering library. Design guidance targets the polygon pets themselves and their visual language.

### Design Principles
1. **Cute over clever** -- Every generated pet should read as a charming character, not an abstract shape experiment. Bias toward combinations that feel endearing.
2. **Geometry is the brand** -- Polygons are not a limitation, they're the identity. Lean into clean edges, flat fills, and visible facets rather than smoothing them away.
3. **Bright and bold** -- Colors should pop. Saturation and value stay high. If a palette combination looks muddy or hostile, it's wrong.
4. **Simple but alive** -- Minimal geometry, maximum personality. Subtle animation (bobbing, squish) and readable features (big eyes, small mouths) make simple shapes feel like creatures.
5. **Variety without chaos** -- Seeds should produce obviously distinct pets, but every result should feel like it belongs to the same species family. Consistency in style, diversity in form.
